SRAM Research Progress

A continuing memory-circuit study from transistor-level behavior to physical implementation.

Functionally validated

6T SRAM bitcell

Built and debugged a transistor-level 6T cell in Cadence. Spectre transient tests at 1.2 V verified write-0, write-1, and hold behavior after correcting the supply setup and NMOS bulk connections.

Schematic-level result

Latch-type sense amplifier

Created the schematic, symbol, and testbench for a latch-type sense amplifier. At TT, 27 °C, and 1.2 V, it resolved both polarities of a 50 mV differential input with approximately 53.6 ps regeneration time. Corner, Monte Carlo, layout, and PEX verification remain future work.

Physical-design study

6T layout topology

Compared Type-1b and thin-cell Type-4 placements, explored PDK-supported shared diffusion, and planned orthogonal bit-line and word-line routing. The Type-4 view is still being iterated; full DRC, LVS, and PEX sign-off has not yet been reached.

System integration

From bitcell to SRAM subsystem

The 6T bitcell and sense amplifier are the first completed circuit units. The next research steps are the precharge unit, row decoder, word-line driver, replica path, timing control, and output buffer, followed by integrated read-operation verification.

Research workflow

Cadence and OpenAccess tooling

Built a reproducible SSH, Cadence SKILL, and OpenAccess workflow to create, inspect, and independently verify SRAM-related schematic and layout cellviews. The tooling supports the research process; it is not presented as a separate competition project.
