Selected Coursework Projects

Power Electronics

Converter and inverter lab portfolio

Built a measurement-to-simulation workflow across oscilloscope fundamentals, rectifiers, DC-DC boost conversion, and single-phase PWM inverter analysis.

  • Measured period, frequency, RMS value, duty cycle, mean value, and TL494 PWM output.
  • Compared half-wave and full-wave rectifiers with capacitor filtering and regulation behavior.
  • Used LTspice to study boost converter duty cycle, ripple, inductor current, and XL6009E1 behavior.
  • Analyzed square-wave, bipolar PWM, and unipolar PWM inverter spectra and harmonic distribution.
Packaging

AI-aided packaging design study

Wrote and presented a study of how AI can support the traditional microelectronics packaging loop from requirements and layout to multi-domain verification.

  • Mapped conventional simulation-driven packaging design flows and their iteration cost.
  • Reviewed electrical concerns including impedance, reflection, crosstalk, SSN, and parasitics.
  • Connected thermal and mechanical analysis to heat conduction, CTE mismatch, stress, and warpage.
  • Positioned AI as a tool for faster exploration, optimization, and design-space screening.
Analog IC Design

Two-stage CMOS operational amplifier

Completed a full analog IC learning path using EDA tools: device characterization, amplifier design, frequency response, layout, DRC, LVS, and tool comparison.

  • Simulated NMOS I-V characteristics across VDS, VGS, process corners, and device sizing.
  • Designed a current-source load common-source amplifier and tuned bias conditions.
  • Evaluated a two-stage op-amp through gain, GBW, phase margin, PSRR, CMRR, and slew-rate simulations.
  • Compared Empyrean Aether and Cadence Virtuoso results, including PDK and process-node effects.

Learning Narrative

A concise map of what the reports show: not only finished assignments, but a growing hardware design method.

Measure first

Started with oscilloscope and waveform literacy: reading time base, calculating frequency, RMS, duty cycle, and mean value, then checking those values against expected circuit behavior.

Model and compare

Used LTspice simulations to connect ideal equations with practical converter behavior, including ripple, harmonics, switching schemes, and losses that appear in real hardware.

Think at system level

Extended beyond circuits into packaging, where electrical, thermal, and mechanical effects interact and design quality depends on multi-physics tradeoffs.

Close the IC loop

Moved from transistor I-V behavior to amplifier design and physical verification, using EDA tools to test whether a schematic can become a manufacturable layout.
